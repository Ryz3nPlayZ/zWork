# zWork Vision

## The problem

AI today lives in browser tabs — ephemeral, siloed from your actual work, disconnected from the files and applications where real productivity happens.

You switch between ChatGPT, your code editor, your notes app, and your terminal. You copy-paste results, lose context between sessions, and repeat the same manual workflows every day. The AI assistant that's supposed to save you time actually adds friction.

## Our thesis

Useful AI should meet you where you work — on your desktop, with access to your files, integrated into your workflows, producing artifacts you can actually use.

We're not building "another AI chatbot." We're building a work companion that:

- **Lives on your computer** — no browser tab required, no context switching
- **Knows your context** — sees your files, understands your projects, remembers your preferences
- **Takes action** — organizes folders, runs commands, automates repetitive tasks
- **Produces outputs** — creates documents, spreadsheets, summaries you can share

## Design principles

### 1. Desktop-first, cloud-optional

Your data and your workflows live on your machine. The cloud should provide value — authentication, telemetry, optional managed features — not be a requirement for basic functionality.

### 2. Chat is the interface, not the product

Streaming text is satisfying but not sufficient. The real value comes from what happens next: documents created, files organized, research synthesized, tasks completed.

### 3. Observable, controllable automation

AI actions should be visible, explainable, and reversible. Users deserve transparency about what's happening, why, and how to stop it.

### 4. Sellable outcomes, not feature lists

Users don't buy "tool calling" or "RAG." They buy "organized downloads folder," "meeting notes follow-up email," "competitor comparison sheet." Ship workflows, not capabilities.

## What success looks like

A user installs zWork, signs in, and within minutes completes a real task that would have taken them 30 minutes manually. They keep using it because it saves actual time, not because it's novel.

### The five-minute test

Can a new user, with zero documentation reading, complete these in five minutes?

1. Install and launch the app
2. Sign in to their account
3. Ask the agent to organize a folder
4. Receive a usable output file

If any step fails or requires troubleshooting, we have work to do.

### North star metrics

- **Time to first useful output** — under 3 minutes from install
- **Task completion rate** — percentage of started tasks that succeed
- **Retention through artifacts** — users return because their work is here
- **Update adoption** — users upgrade because they trust the product

## What we're not building

- **Not a browser extension** — native desktop access matters
- **Not just another chat UI** — conversation is a means, not an end
- **Not a replacement for ChatGPT** — we're for getting work done, not entertainment
- **Not an automation platform for everyone** — focused on individual productivity use cases

## The path forward

Near-term, we're focused on the artifact workspace — turning chat into a surface that produces, edits, and organizes deliverables. Documents, spreadsheets, charts, tasks. Things you can touch, share, and build on.

Long-term, we see zWork as the interface layer between human intent and machine action — the place where you describe what you want, and the system figures out how to get it done, using all the tools and services already on your machine.
